## Overview

FXCM (Forex Capital Markets) is one of the longest-running names in retail forex, founded in New York in 1999. The broker's history is marked by both pioneering contributions to retail forex and a significant regulatory controversy — the 2017 NFA ban that forced its exit from the US market and subsequent restructuring under Jefferies Financial Group.

Today, FXCM operates under triple Tier-1 regulation (FCA, ASIC, CySEC) with a genuine agency execution model. Under this model, FXCM routes orders to external liquidity providers and earns revenue from commissions and markups rather than from client losses — aligning the broker's interests with trader success.

The Active Trader account offers competitive raw spread pricing (from 0.2 pips EUR/USD with $3/lot commission), while the Standard account embeds costs in wider spreads (1.0 pips). The TradingView integration, launched in 2022, adds advanced charting capabilities to FXCM's execution infrastructure.

FXCM offers 400+ instruments focused on forex, indices, commodities, and crypto CFDs. The instrument range is modest, but the agency execution model and transparent pricing make FXCM appealing for traders who prioritize knowing that their broker isn't trading against them.

## Account Intro

FXCM offers two main account types. The Standard account provides commission-free trading with wider spreads, while the Active Trader account offers raw pricing for high-volume traders.

## Account Outro

Demo accounts are free with $50,000 virtual funds. Islamic accounts are available. There is no mid-tier option between Standard and Active Trader, which creates a significant jump at the $25,000 threshold. Micro lots are available for position sizing flexibility.

## Regulation

FXCM holds triple Tier-1 regulation: FCA (217689), ASIC (309763), and CySEC (392/20). The FCA license is the primary regulatory jurisdiction with FSCS protection up to £85,000. Client funds are segregated under all entities with negative balance protection.

The 2017 NFA ban is a permanent mark on FXCM's history. The current operation under Jefferies Financial Group ownership, combined with FCA/ASIC/CySEC oversight, provides strong contemporary regulation. Traders should weigh the historical context against the current regulatory standing.

## Costs

FXCM's Standard account offers spread-only pricing with EUR/USD averaging 1.0 pips — a total cost of $10.00 per standard lot. This is wider than many competitors and represents the main cost concern for standard account holders.

The Active Trader account ($25,000 minimum) provides raw spreads averaging 0.30 pips with a $3/lot commission ($6 round turn). Total cost is approximately $9.00 per lot — competitive for an agency-model broker though higher than pure ECN competitors.

FXCM charges no deposit or withdrawal fees. No inactivity fees apply. The clean fee structure outside of trading costs is a positive. The agency model means FXCM has no incentive to manipulate pricing against clients.

## Deposits

FXCM accepts deposits from $50 via cards, Skrill, and bank/wire transfer. All methods are free. Cards and Skrill are processed instantly. Bank transfers take 1-3 business days.

In our testing, a £200 card deposit was credited within 30 seconds. A £100 bank withdrawal was processed within 2 business days. FXCM supports GBP, USD, EUR, AUD, CHF, JPY, and other currencies as account bases.

## Platforms

Trading Station is FXCM's proprietary platform featuring unique tools: the Forex Market Scanner identifies trading opportunities across multiple pairs simultaneously, FXCM Signals provides automated trading signals, and real-time execution analytics show your trading statistics.

MetaTrader 4 is available with full EA support and custom indicators. The TradingView integration allows execution directly from TradingView's advanced charting interface — combining FXCM's agency execution with one of the best charting platforms in the industry.

FXCM does not offer MT5 or cTrader. The platform suite covers three execution environments (Trading Station, MT4, TradingView) — sufficient for most traders but lacking the breadth of brokers like FxPro (4 platforms) or Pepperstone (4 platforms).

## Mobile

Trading Station's mobile app provides charting, order management, and trading signal notifications on iOS and Android. The app is functional with clean design and responsive data loading.

For advanced mobile charting, TradingView's mobile app can be used alongside FXCM's execution — though this requires switching between apps. The native Trading Station app provides a more integrated but less feature-rich mobile experience.

## Support

FXCM provides 24/5 customer support via phone, email, and live chat. Support is available from London, Sydney, and other offices. Our testing found live chat wait times averaging 5-12 minutes with knowledgeable agents.

The FXCM Help Center provides extensive documentation, platform tutorials, and market education. Support quality has improved under Jefferies ownership, though the smaller client base means less user community content than larger brokers.

## Education

FXCM provides educational content through FXCM Insights — covering forex basics, trading strategies, and market analysis. The education offering is adequate but not as structured or comprehensive as IG Academy or XM.

FXCM's unique educational value comes from its transparency: published execution statistics, the agency model explanation, and real-time market scanner help traders understand market dynamics beyond theoretical education.

## Trustpilot

FXCM has a 3.3/5 Trustpilot rating from about 2,640 verified reviews. The lower score partially reflects historical reputation damage from the 2017 NFA ban. Recent reviews are more positive, praising the agency model and Active Trader pricing. Negative reviews mention wider Standard account spreads and the historical controversy. The score is below average for a triple Tier-1 regulated broker.

## Verdict

FXCM's agency execution model is its defining value proposition — the broker genuinely routes orders to liquidity providers and earns from volume, not losses. For traders who have experienced or are concerned about dealing desk manipulation, FXCM offers transparent, conflict-free execution backed by triple Tier-1 regulation and Jefferies institutional ownership.

The baggage is real: the 2017 NFA ban, a below-average Trustpilot score, and wider Standard account spreads create legitimate concerns. For pure execution cost, IC Markets and Pepperstone are superior. But for traders who prioritize broker transparency and the agency model above all else, FXCM provides a unique proposition in the retail forex space.
