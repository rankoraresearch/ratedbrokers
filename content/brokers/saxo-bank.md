## Overview

Saxo Bank is a Danish investment bank and multi-asset broker offering the widest instrument selection in the retail trading industry — over 71,000 products across forex, stocks, ETFs, bonds, futures, options, and mutual funds. Founded in 1992 by Kim Fournais and Lars Seier Christensen, Saxo holds a full Danish banking license and has evolved into a premium financial institution serving 1.2 million+ clients.

What fundamentally distinguishes Saxo Bank is its positioning as an investment platform rather than just a forex broker. While competitors focus primarily on forex and CFDs, Saxo provides direct market access to 40+ global exchanges, enabling real stock ownership, bond trading, and futures execution alongside traditional CFD products.

The SaxoTraderPRO desktop platform is widely considered the best proprietary trading platform available to retail clients, with institutional-grade features including 50+ order types, algorithmic execution, portfolio analytics, and integrated research from the renowned Saxo Strategy team. SaxoTraderGO provides a streamlined web experience.

Saxo's regulatory profile includes four Tier-1 licenses: DFSA (Denmark, with banking license), FCA (UK), ASIC (Australia), and MAS (Singapore). The company was valued at approximately $8 billion when Geely Group acquired a majority stake in 2022, reflecting its institutional credibility.

## Account Intro

Saxo offers a tiered pricing structure with three levels. Each tier provides progressively tighter spreads and lower commissions, rewarding higher account balances and trading volumes.

## Account Outro

Saxo also offers SaxoInvestor for managed portfolio solutions and robo-advisory. Islamic accounts are available in select regions. Demo accounts provide $100,000 in virtual funds across all platform versions. Corporate and institutional accounts are available.

## Regulation

Saxo Bank's regulatory profile is among the strongest in the industry. The DFSA banking license (1149) subjects Saxo to banking-level capital requirements, depositor protection, and regular stress testing. This is a higher regulatory standard than any standard broker license.

Additional Tier-1 licenses from FCA, ASIC, and MAS provide global coverage. UK clients benefit from FSCS protection up to £85,000. The $100/quarter inactivity fee and $2,000 minimum deposit reflect Saxo's premium positioning rather than regulatory requirements.

## Costs

Saxo's Classic tier offers EUR/USD from 0.6 pips with a $3 per $100,000 commission — a total cost of approximately $9.00 per standard lot. This is higher than ECN brokers but includes access to the premium SaxoTrader platform and research.

Platinum tier ($200K+ equity) reduces spreads to 0.4 pips and commissions to $2.50/100K. VIP tier ($1M+) further reduces to 0.3 pips and $2/100K, with dedicated relationship managers and priority support.

Additional costs include exchange data fees (real-time data for DMA), custody fees for stock holdings, and the $100/quarter inactivity fee. The fee structure is more complex than typical forex brokers but comparable to full-service investment platforms.

## Deposits

Saxo requires a $2,000 minimum deposit, the highest among mainstream brokers. Deposits via card and bank transfer are free. Wire transfers are free for the initial deposit but may carry bank fees from the sending institution.

In our testing, a card deposit of $5,000 was credited within 1 hour. Bank transfer deposits took 2 business days. Withdrawals were processed within 1-2 business days. Saxo supports 26 base currencies.

## Platforms

SaxoTraderGO is Saxo's web-based platform with a clean, modern interface, 50+ indicators, integrated research, and responsive design. It's powerful enough for most traders while remaining accessible. Available on any browser without installation.

SaxoTraderPRO is the institutional-grade desktop platform with 50+ order types, multi-screen layouts, algo execution, depth of market, and advanced portfolio analytics. It's one of the most sophisticated platforms available to retail traders.

SaxoInvestor is a simplified platform for managed investing, offering robo-advisory services, model portfolios, and ESG-screened investment options. There is no MT4, MT5, cTrader, or TradingView — Saxo uses exclusively its proprietary platform ecosystem.

## Mobile

SaxoTraderGO mobile is available on iOS and Android with the full functionality of the web platform. Advanced charting, order management, and integrated research make it one of the best mobile trading experiences available.

The app supports complex order types, watchlists, real-time alerts, and account management. Portfolio overview and performance tracking are well-implemented, reflecting Saxo's focus on the investment experience beyond pure trading.

## Support

Saxo provides 24/5 support via phone, email, and live chat. VIP and Platinum clients receive dedicated relationship managers and priority access. Standard support quality is professional but not always fast — wait times of 5-15 minutes are common.

The Saxo support center provides extensive documentation, FAQs, and video guides. Platform onboarding assistance is available for new clients. The support experience reflects Saxo's positioning as a premium service rather than a high-volume retail operation.

## Education

Saxo provides solid educational content through Saxo Academy, covering trading basics, platform tutorials, and investment strategies. The content quality is good but less comprehensive than IG Academy or XM's educational hub.

Research is Saxo's standout strength. The Saxo Strategy team, led by Chief Investment Officer Steen Jakobsen, produces daily market analysis, quarterly investment outlooks, and the widely-followed annual 'Outrageous Predictions' report. The research quality rivals institutional-level output.

## Trustpilot

Saxo Bank has a 3.8/5 Trustpilot rating from about 6,400 verified reviews — lower than many competitors. Negative reviews often cite the $2,000 minimum deposit, $100/quarter inactivity fee, and complex fee structure. Positive reviews praise the platform quality, instrument range, and research. The lower score reflects Saxo's premium pricing model and the expectations gap for traders accustomed to no-minimum brokers.

## Country

Saxo Bank accepts clients from 170+ countries. Entity assignment: DFSA (Denmark/EU), FCA (UK), ASIC (Australia), MAS (Singapore), or various international entities. US clients are not accepted. Product availability varies by jurisdiction due to local regulations.

## Verdict

Saxo Bank earns our Best for Professionals award for 2026. For serious traders and investors who want access to 71,000+ instruments, DMA on global exchanges, institutional-quality platforms, and world-class research, Saxo is unmatched. The Danish banking license provides safety beyond any standard broker regulation.

The $2,000 minimum deposit, complex fees, and premium pricing make Saxo unsuitable for beginners or cost-sensitive traders. For pure forex with tight spreads, IC Markets or Pepperstone offer much better value. But for multi-asset investors who want everything on one platform with banking-level protection, Saxo Bank is the gold standard.
