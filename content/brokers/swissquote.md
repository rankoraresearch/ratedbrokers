## Overview

Swissquote is a Swiss online bank listed on the SIX Swiss Exchange (SQN), regulated by FINMA under a full Swiss banking license. This makes it one of the safest places to trade and invest in the world — client funds are protected under Swiss banking law with deposit protection up to CHF 100,000, a standard that exceeds any broker-level regulation.

What sets Swissquote apart from other premium brokers is its nature as a genuine bank. Beyond trading, it offers savings accounts, debit cards, multi-currency accounts in 35+ currencies, mortgages, and robo-advisory services. The integration of banking and trading creates a financial ecosystem that pure brokers cannot replicate.

The instrument range is staggering: over 3 million tradable products including direct exchange access to Swiss, US, European, and Asian stock markets. Forex (80+ pairs), CFDs, ETFs, bonds, options, futures, structured products, and cryptocurrencies are all accessible. Swissquote was the first Swiss bank to offer crypto trading in 2018.

The trade-off for Swiss banking safety is pricing. Standard account spreads of 1.3 pips on EUR/USD are significantly wider than ECN competitors. The Premium ($10,000) and Prime ($50,000) tiers offer progressively tighter pricing but remain above ECN levels. Swissquote's value proposition is safety and breadth, not raw cost.

## Account Intro

Swissquote offers three trading tiers with progressively tighter spreads. Each tier shares the same platforms and instrument access, with the difference being pricing and minimum balance requirements.

## Account Outro

Demo accounts are available with virtual funds. Islamic accounts are not available. The Invest Easy robo-advisory service operates alongside trading accounts. Corporate, institutional, and white-label solutions are offered for business clients.

## Regulation

Swissquote holds a FINMA Swiss banking license — the gold standard of financial regulation. Under Swiss banking law, client deposits are protected up to CHF 100,000 per depositor, and the bank is subject to mandatory capital adequacy requirements, regular stress testing, and FINMA oversight. This exceeds the protection provided by any standard broker license.

Additional Tier-1 licenses from FCA (562170) and MAS (CMS100502) extend Swiss-quality protection to UK and Singapore clients. The SIX Swiss Exchange listing (SQN) adds public market transparency. Swissquote manages over CHF 60 billion in client assets, demonstrating institutional scale.

## Costs

Swissquote's Standard account offers EUR/USD from 1.3 pips with no commission — a total cost of $13.00 per standard lot. This is significantly wider than ECN brokers and premium market makers like IG (0.6 pips). The higher cost reflects Swiss banking overhead and infrastructure.

The Premium account ($10,000 minimum) reduces EUR/USD to approximately 0.6 pips, competitive with IG and CMC Markets. The Prime account ($50,000) offers raw spreads from 0.0 pips with a $2.50/lot commission, bringing costs closer to ECN levels.

Additional costs include custody fees for stock holdings, real-time market data subscriptions, and exchange access fees. The fee structure is more complex than typical forex brokers but comparable to full-service banking platforms. No inactivity fees are charged.

## Deposits

Swissquote's Swiss entity accepts deposits primarily via bank transfer and wire transfer. No credit card or e-wallet deposits are available on the main Swiss entity — this reflects banking norms rather than broker conventions. UK (FCA) entity supports additional methods.

In our testing, an eBanking transfer of CHF 2,000 was credited within 2 hours. International wire transfers took 2 business days. Swissquote supports 35+ base currencies, reflecting its banking heritage and multi-currency account capabilities.

## Platforms

Advanced Trader is Swissquote's proprietary platform providing clean charting, order management, and integrated research. While functional, it lacks the polish and feature depth of IG's platform or CMC's Next Generation.

MetaTrader 4 and MT5 are available for forex and CFD trading with standard functionality. The MT offering is solid but without the enhanced add-ons found at brokers like Admirals (Supreme Edition).

The real platform strength is Swissquote's eBanking interface — a unified portal for trading, banking, savings, and portfolio management. Invest Easy robo-advisory provides automated portfolio construction and rebalancing for passive investors.

## Mobile

Swissquote's mobile app combines trading and banking in one interface — trade forex, manage your savings account, check your debit card balance, and monitor portfolios from a single app. This integration is unique in the industry.

The trading section provides charting, order management, and watchlists on iOS and Android. The banking integration adds bill payments, currency conversion, and account management. While the trading charting is less advanced than dedicated trading apps, the all-in-one banking experience is unmatched.

## Support

Swissquote provides customer support via phone, email, and live chat during Swiss business hours. Multi-language support covers English, French, German, Italian, and other languages. Our testing found phone support to be the most responsive channel.

As a Swiss bank, support quality is professional and knowledgeable, particularly for banking and account-related inquiries. Trading-specific support is adequate but less specialized than at dedicated forex brokers. Response times average 5-15 minutes.

## Education

Swissquote provides educational content through its Learn section, covering trading basics, market analysis, and investment strategies. The content is oriented toward investors rather than active traders, reflecting the banking heritage.

The Swissquote Magazine provides quarterly investment insights, market outlooks, and interviews with industry leaders. Research quality is solid with daily technical and fundamental analysis. The education offering targets informed investors rather than complete beginners.

## Verdict

Swissquote is the ultimate safety-first choice for traders and investors. The FINMA Swiss banking license, SIX Swiss Exchange listing, and CHF 100,000 deposit protection provide security that no standard broker can match. The 3 million+ tradable products and integrated banking services create a financial ecosystem unique in the industry.

The cost is high. Standard spreads of 1.3 pips and a $1,000 minimum deposit make Swissquote unsuitable for cost-sensitive forex traders. For competitive pricing, IG or Pepperstone are far better choices. But for high-net-worth individuals, Swiss residents, and anyone who prioritizes maximum safety, Swissquote combines banking-grade protection with comprehensive market access.
