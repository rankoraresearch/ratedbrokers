## Overview

eToro is the world's leading social trading platform, serving 40 million+ registered users across 140+ countries. Founded in 2007 in Tel Aviv by brothers Yoni and Ronen Assia along with David Ring, the company pioneered the concept of CopyTrader — allowing users to automatically replicate the trades of successful investors.

What makes eToro fundamentally different from traditional brokers is its social-first approach. The platform functions like a financial social network where traders share strategies, publish analyses, and build followings. CopyTrader transforms this social layer into actionable investing — you can allocate funds to mirror any trader's portfolio with one click.

eToro offers 6,000+ instruments including real stocks (with actual ownership, not CFDs), 70+ cryptocurrencies, forex (49 pairs), indices, commodities, ETFs, and Smart Portfolios. The zero-commission real stock trading for non-US clients has made eToro a competitor to traditional stock brokerages as well as forex brokers.

The platform is regulated by three Tier-1 authorities: FCA (UK), CySEC (Cyprus), and ASIC (Australia). eToro's pre-IPO valuation reached $3.5 billion, reflecting institutional confidence in the platform's growth model. Client funds are segregated and protected under each regulatory entity.

## Account Intro

eToro offers a straightforward account structure. All retail clients access the same platform with the same spreads. Professional clients can apply for higher leverage. The eToro Money account provides crypto wallet and currency management services.

## Account Outro

eToro's $100,000 virtual portfolio allows risk-free practice. There are no separate ECN or raw spread accounts — eToro's model is built around social trading rather than tight-spread execution. Islamic accounts are not available.

## Regulation

eToro holds three Tier-1 regulatory licenses: FCA (583263), CySEC (109/10), and ASIC (491139). Each entity provides segregated client funds, negative balance protection, and investor compensation scheme access. The FCA entity covers UK clients with FSCS protection up to £85,000.

eToro's regulatory standing is reinforced by its size (40M+ users), institutional backing (SoftBank, Wellington Management investors), and preparation for public listing. The company publishes regular financial disclosures and maintains transparent operations across all regulated entities.

## Costs

eToro's forex costs are higher than ECN brokers, with EUR/USD averaging 1.0 pips. This reflects the market maker model and the embedded cost of the social trading infrastructure. For forex-focused traders, this is a significant premium over IC Markets (0.02 pips) or Pepperstone (0.10 pips).

The standout cost advantage is zero-commission real stock and ETF trading for non-US, non-leveraged positions. This makes eToro competitive with dedicated stock brokerages like Trading 212 or Robinhood while offering multi-asset access on one platform.

eToro charges a $5 withdrawal fee, a conversion fee of 0.5%-1.5% on non-USD deposits, and overnight fees on leveraged CFD positions. These fees add up for active forex traders and are a legitimate concern.

Traders who primarily want forex execution should consider ECN alternatives; those who value social features and multi-asset access may find eToro's pricing acceptable.

## Deposits

eToro accepts deposits from $50 via cards, PayPal, Skrill, Neteller, and bank transfer. All deposit methods are free except wire transfer which requires a $500 minimum. Most electronic methods are credited instantly.

All deposits are converted to USD (eToro's base currency) with a conversion fee of 0.5%-1.5% depending on the currency. This is an important cost consideration for non-US traders who fund in EUR, GBP, or other currencies.

## Platforms

The eToro platform is a proprietary web and mobile application that cannot be found elsewhere. It combines trading, social networking, copy trading, and portfolio management in one interface. The social feed shows real-time trades, analyses, and discussions from the 40M+ user community.

CopyTrader is the flagship feature — select a trader to copy, allocate funds, and their positions are replicated in your account proportionally. CopyTrader includes risk management tools like Stop Loss on the copied trader's equity. Smart Portfolios provide themed, automatically rebalanced investment strategies.

eToro does not support MT4, MT5, cTrader, or TradingView. The proprietary-only approach means traders cannot use third-party EAs, indicators, or charting tools. This is a significant limitation for advanced technical traders but makes sense for eToro's target market of social and copy traders.

## Mobile

The eToro mobile app is excellent, providing the full platform experience on iOS and Android. The social feed, CopyTrader, charting, order management, and portfolio overview are all seamlessly integrated. The app regularly ranks among the top finance apps in both stores.

Push notifications keep users informed of social activity, price alerts, and copied trader actions. The mobile experience is arguably eToro's strongest platform implementation, with smooth performance and intuitive navigation optimized for touch interfaces.

## Support

eToro offers customer support via a ticket/help desk system, live chat, and phone. Support quality can be inconsistent due to the large user base — wait times of 10-30 minutes are not uncommon during peak periods. The Help Center with searchable FAQ articles is comprehensive.

For Club members (based on equity tiers), eToro provides dedicated account managers and priority support. The customer service experience is a known weakness relative to specialized forex brokers like Pepperstone or IC Markets.

## Education

eToro Academy provides educational content including trading guides, video tutorials, and webinars covering forex, stocks, crypto, and investing basics. The content is beginner-oriented and well-produced, though not as comprehensive as IG Academy or XM's educational hub.

The social feed itself serves as an informal educational resource — beginner traders can follow experienced investors, read their analyses, and learn from their trading decisions. This social learning model is unique to eToro and arguably more engaging than traditional educational content.

## Trustpilot

eToro has a 4.4/5 Trustpilot rating from over 30,843+ verified reviews. Positive reviews highlight CopyTrader, the user interface, and zero-commission stocks. Negative reviews focus on the $5 withdrawal fee, forex spreads, customer support wait times, and conversion fees. The polarized reviews reflect eToro's position as a social platform rather than a trading-first broker.

## Country

eToro accepts clients from 140+ countries. Entity assignment: FCA (UK), CySEC (EU/EEA), ASIC (Australia), or eToro USA (US — limited to crypto). Notable exclusions include Canada, Japan, Turkey, and India. US clients can only access cryptocurrency trading on eToro, not forex or stocks.

## Verdict

eToro earns our Best for Copy Trading award for 2026. If your primary goal is to copy successful traders, build a diversified portfolio with zero-commission stocks, and participate in a trading community, eToro is unmatched. The CopyTrader feature, 6,000+ instruments, and social ecosystem create a unique investment experience not available elsewhere.

However, for pure forex trading, eToro is not the best choice. The 1.0 pip EUR/USD spread, $5 withdrawal fee, and lack of MT4/MT5 make it suboptimal for active forex traders and scalpers. If you want social features with better forex pricing, consider NAGA. For pure forex, IC Markets or Pepperstone are vastly superior.
