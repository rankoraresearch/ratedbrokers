## Overview

OANDA is one of the most venerable names in online forex trading, founded in 1996 by Dr. Michael Stumm and Dr. Richard Olsen. Operating for 28 years, it was among the first companies to offer free currency exchange rate data on the internet and pioneered web-based retail forex trading with the launch of fxTrade in 2001.

As one of only two forex brokers legally accepting US clients (alongside IG), OANDA holds NFA/CFTC regulation — the strictest regulatory framework for forex in the world. This makes OANDA the go-to choice for American traders who have extremely limited broker options due to Dodd-Frank regulations.

OANDA offers 120+ tradable instruments focused primarily on forex (70+ pairs), with additional coverage of indices, commodities, and bonds. The instrument range is smaller than most competitors, reflecting OANDA's traditional focus on currency markets. Trading is available on the proprietary OANDA Trade platform, MT4, and TradingView.

The broker's regulatory profile includes four Tier-1 licenses: NFA (US), FCA (UK), ASIC (Australia), and MAS (Singapore). Combined with 28 years of clean operating history and published execution statistics, OANDA offers a level of transparency and safety that few competitors can match.

## Account Intro

OANDA offers a simple account structure with two main tiers. The Standard account provides no-minimum-deposit access for all traders, while the Premium account offers tighter spreads for clients maintaining $20,000+ in equity.

## Account Outro

OANDA does not offer MT5, cTrader, or commission-based ECN accounts. Islamic accounts are available for swap-free trading. Demo accounts are free and unlimited. The API Trading option appeals to algorithmic traders with full REST and FIX protocol access.

## Regulation

OANDA holds four Tier-1 regulatory licenses, making it one of the most heavily regulated brokers in the world. The NFA/CFTC registration provides US clients with the strictest forex regulatory protections available, including mandatory capital requirements, regular audits, and transparent pricing.

FCA regulation covers UK and European clients with FSCS protection up to £85,000. ASIC and MAS licenses extend Tier-1 protections to Australian and Southeast Asian clients. OANDA publishes quarterly execution statistics including fill rates, average spreads, and slippage data.

## Costs

OANDA's standard account offers spread-only pricing averaging 1.0 pips on EUR/USD — wider than ECN competitors but in line with market maker peers like IG. There are no commissions, making cost calculation simple: total cost equals the spread.

The Premium account ($20,000 minimum equity) reduces EUR/USD spreads to approximately 0.6 pips, comparable to IG's standard pricing. OANDA is one of the few brokers to offer fractional pip pricing, providing greater precision in execution and cost.

OANDA charges no deposit fees (except $20 for wire transfers), no withdrawal fees (except wires), no inactivity fees, and no account maintenance fees. The clean fee structure is appreciated by traders who value transparency.

## Deposits

OANDA offers $0 minimum deposits with free ACH transfers for US clients, free card deposits, and free PayPal. Wire transfers carry a $20 fee. Most electronic methods are processed instantly.

## Platforms

The OANDA Trade platform is a modern, web-based proprietary platform with excellent charting (65+ indicators), integrated research, customizable layouts, and fast execution. It represents a significant upgrade from the legacy fxTrade platform.

MetaTrader 4 is available for traders who prefer EA trading and the familiar MT4 environment. The TradingView integration, launched in 2022, allows advanced charting and order execution directly from TradingView's interface.

OANDA's REST API (v20) and FIX API are among the best in the industry for algorithmic trading. The API provides real-time streaming pricing, order management, historical data, and account analytics. Python, Java, and JavaScript libraries are available with extensive documentation.

## Mobile

OANDA's mobile app provides a clean, intuitive experience on iOS and Android with full trading functionality, 50+ indicators, customizable watchlists, and push notifications. The app is well-reviewed in both app stores.

The mobile experience mirrors the web platform's capabilities with responsive charting, one-tap trading, and integrated economic calendar. For US traders with limited broker options, OANDA's mobile app provides one of the best on-the-go trading experiences.

## Support

OANDA offers 24/5 customer support via live chat, email, and phone from offices in New York, London, Singapore, and Tokyo. Our testing found live chat wait times averaging 3-7 minutes with knowledgeable agents.

Support quality is generally good but can be slower during peak US trading hours. Email responses averaged 8 hours. OANDA also maintains an extensive Help Center with searchable articles and video guides.

OANDA provides moderate educational content including trading tutorials, webinars, and market analysis. The OANDA MarketPulse blog delivers daily fundamental and technical analysis from in-house analysts.

The educational offering is adequate but doesn't match the structured learning platforms of IG Academy or XM. Research tools include an economic calendar, historical rate converter, and currency correlation tool — useful for fundamental analysis.
