## Overview

Dukascopy Bank SA is a Geneva-based Swiss bank that operates one of the most unique trading models in retail forex — the SWFX (Swiss FX Marketplace). Unlike typical ECN brokers that aggregate liquidity from providers, SWFX functions as a centralized marketplace where all participants — banks, hedge funds, and retail traders — interact in a transparent, visible order book environment.

Founded in 2004 and granted a FINMA Swiss banking license in 2010, Dukascopy combines institutional-grade ECN execution with Swiss banking safety. Client deposits are protected under Swiss banking law up to CHF 100,000 — the same standard that protects deposits at UBS or Credit Suisse.

The JForex 4 platform is designed for serious traders and algorithm developers. Supporting Java-based strategy programming, visual strategy building, historical tick data backtesting, and multi-account management, JForex caters to a technical audience that values execution transparency and algorithmic capabilities over user-friendly design.

Dukascopy offers 1,200+ instruments with a focus on forex (60+ pairs), indices, commodities, and crypto CFDs. The instrument range is smaller than major competitors, reflecting the broker's specialization in forex ECN execution rather than multi-asset breadth.

## Account Intro

Dukascopy offers a straightforward account structure with the same ECN execution across all tiers. The difference is commission level based on deposit/balance tier.

## Account Outro

Demo accounts are available with virtual funds and full JForex/MT4 access. Islamic swap-free accounts can be arranged. Managed accounts and white-label solutions are available for institutional clients. Binary options accounts operate under separate conditions.

## Regulation

Dukascopy Bank SA holds a FINMA Swiss banking license — the highest regulatory standard available in the financial industry. Under Swiss banking law, client deposits are protected up to CHF 100,000 per depositor through the esisuisse depositor protection scheme. The bank is subject to mandatory capital adequacy requirements, regular FINMA audits, and stress testing.

Unlike brokers regulated by FCA or ASIC (which provide broker-level protection), Dukascopy's banking license means it operates under the same regulatory framework as major Swiss banks. This provides a level of safety that is unmatched by any non-banking broker entity.

## Costs

Dukascopy's ECN pricing starts with raw spreads averaging 0.20 pips on EUR/USD with a $3.50 per lot commission ($7 round turn). Total cost per standard lot is approximately $9.00 — higher than IC Markets ($6.20) but includes Swiss banking protection.

Volume-based discounts reduce commissions progressively. The Premium tier ($100,000+ balance) offers $2.50/lot commission ($5 round turn), bringing total costs to approximately $7.00 per lot — more competitive at higher volumes.

Card deposits carry a 1.2% fee — unusual in the modern broker landscape. Bank transfers are free. No inactivity fees or account maintenance charges apply. The fee structure reflects banking conventions rather than typical broker models.

## Deposits

Dukascopy accepts deposits via bank transfer (free), wire transfer (free), credit/debit card (1.2% fee), and cryptocurrency (BTC, free). The $1,000 minimum reflects the Swiss banking positioning. Card deposit fees are an unusual cost in the industry.

In our testing, a CHF 2,000 bank transfer was credited within 2 business days. A Bitcoin deposit was credited after 3 network confirmations (approximately 45 minutes). Supported base currencies include CHF, USD, EUR, GBP, JPY, CAD, AUD, and PLN.

## Platforms

JForex 4 is Dukascopy's flagship platform — a Java-based application with advanced features including multi-chart layouts, 250+ indicators, SWFX depth of market, and comprehensive strategy development tools. The visual strategy builder allows algorithmic trading without coding knowledge.

The strategy backtesting engine uses historical tick data (bid/ask) with variable spreads and commissions, providing more realistic backtesting than most platforms. Multi-account management through JForex allows professional traders to manage multiple strategies simultaneously.

MetaTrader 4 is available as an alternative, connecting to SWFX for execution. The MT4 implementation provides familiar tools with Dukascopy's ECN pricing. MT5, cTrader, and TradingView are not supported — JForex is the primary platform investment.

## Mobile

Dukascopy's mobile app provides trading access on iOS and Android with charting, order management, and SWFX market depth. The app is functional but less polished than mobile offerings from IG or Pepperstone.

The mobile experience supports standard order types, watchlists, and real-time data. However, the advanced features that define the JForex desktop experience (strategy development, backtesting) are not available on mobile.

## Support

Dukascopy offers customer support via phone, email, and live chat during Swiss business hours (extended for trading hours). Support is available in English, French, German, and Russian. Our testing found phone support responsive with knowledgeable agents.

As a Swiss bank, support quality is professional but oriented toward institutional-style communication. Response times average 3-10 minutes during European hours. The Help Center provides documentation for JForex, API, and account management.

## Education

Dukascopy provides research-oriented content through its FXSpider news aggregator, Dukascopy TV (financial video channel), and the annual Dukascopy Community contest for traders. Educational content for beginners is limited.

The JForex Wiki provides comprehensive documentation for platform usage and strategy development. The broker targets experienced traders and developers who are self-directed learners rather than education-dependent beginners.

Dukascopy accepts clients from 100+ countries through its Swiss entity. US clients are not accepted. EU, UK, and international clients are onboarded under the FINMA-regulated Swiss banking entity. Leverage and product availability vary by client jurisdiction and classification.

## Verdict

Dukascopy is the choice for traders who prioritize Swiss banking safety above all else while demanding genuine ECN execution. The SWFX marketplace provides unmatched transparency with visible order books, and the JForex platform offers serious algorithmic trading capabilities. FINMA protection exceeds anything available from non-banking brokers.

The trade-offs are real: JForex has a steep learning curve, the instrument range is limited, card deposits carry a 1.2% fee, and the $1,000 minimum excludes casual traders. For raw execution cost, IC Markets is cheaper. For platform usability, Pepperstone is better. But for Swiss banking safety with ECN pricing, Dukascopy occupies a unique and valuable niche.
