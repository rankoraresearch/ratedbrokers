## Overview

Fusion Markets is a Melbourne-based ECN broker built on a single premise: offer the lowest trading costs possible while maintaining ASIC regulation. Founded in 2017 by industry veterans Phil Horner and Matthew Murphie, the company has rapidly grown its reputation as the cheapest ASIC-regulated ECN broker available.

The ZERO account charges just $4.50 per lot ($9 round turn) — significantly less than IC Markets ($6/lot) or Pepperstone ($7/lot). Combined with average EUR/USD spreads of 0.09 pips, total trading cost is approximately $5.40 per standard lot — the lowest from any ASIC-regulated broker we've tested.

Despite the low costs, Fusion Markets doesn't compromise on platform quality. MetaTrader 4, MetaTrader 5, cTrader, and TradingView are all available, providing comprehensive coverage for manual traders, algorithmic strategies, and chart-focused analysis. The $0 minimum deposit removes any entry barrier.

The 4.5/5 Trustpilot score from 2,180+ reviews confirms that the cost-focused proposition delivers genuine value. Clients consistently praise the transparent pricing, fast execution, and straightforward approach. For traders where cost minimization is the primary concern, Fusion Markets is a compelling choice.

## Account Intro

Fusion Markets keeps it simple with two account types — Classic (spread-inclusive, no commission) and ZERO (raw spreads + $4.50/lot commission). Both have $0 minimum deposit. The difference is purely in pricing model.

## Account Outro

Demo accounts are available across all platforms. Islamic swap-free accounts can be requested. Myfxbook AutoTrade copy trading is available for both account types. No tiered VIP program — every client gets the same low rates.

## Regulation

Fusion Markets is regulated by ASIC (385620) — Australia's Tier-1 financial regulator providing strong client protection including mandatory fund segregation, regulatory capital requirements, and access to AFCA for dispute resolution. Australian clients are well-protected.

International clients may be assigned to VFSC Vanuatu (40256) or FSA Seychelles (SD096) — Tier-3 jurisdictions offering significantly less protection. Fusion Markets voluntarily maintains segregated funds across entities. The relatively short operating history (since 2017) means less long-term track record than established competitors.

## Costs

Fusion Markets' ZERO account offers the lowest ECN commission from any ASIC-regulated broker: $4.50/lot ($9 round turn). With EUR/USD averaging 0.09 pips, total cost is approximately $5.40 per standard lot. For comparison: IC Markets costs $6.20/lot, Pepperstone $8.00/lot, and FP Markets $7.00/lot.

The Classic account provides commission-free trading with spreads from 0.9 pips — approximately $9.00 per lot on EUR/USD. This is competitive for a commission-free model and slightly better than many competitors' standard accounts.

No deposit fees, no withdrawal fees, no inactivity fee. The cost structure is genuinely transparent. Swap rates are standard for the ECN model. The absence of any hidden charges is a core part of Fusion Markets' value proposition.

## Deposits

Fusion Markets accepts deposits from $0 via cards, Skrill, Neteller, bank transfer, and cryptocurrency. All methods are free. Electronic deposits process instantly; bank transfers take 1-3 days. The $0 minimum applies across all methods and account types.

In our testing, card deposits credited in under 15 seconds. Withdrawals via Skrill processed within 24 hours. Fusion Markets supports AUD, USD, EUR, GBP, SGD, and JPY as base currencies.

## Platforms

MetaTrader 4 and MT5 provide the standard forex and multi-asset trading experience with EA support. cTrader adds depth-of-market, Level II pricing, and cAlgo for algorithmic trading. The combination covers the core needs of most traders.

TradingView integration enables advanced charting with direct execution from one of the world's most popular charting platforms. This is a valuable addition that not all ECN brokers offer.

No proprietary platform is available — Fusion Markets relies entirely on established third-party platforms. This keeps development costs low (contributing to lower commissions) while ensuring traders have access to well-tested, feature-rich platforms.

## Mobile

Standard MT4/MT5 and cTrader mobile apps provide full mobile trading capability. TradingView's mobile app offers charting and analysis. The mobile experience is functional through these established apps.

No proprietary mobile app exists — all mobile trading goes through third-party platform apps. This is adequate for most traders but lacks the customized mobile experience offered by brokers like Capital.com or eToro.

## Support

Fusion Markets provides 24/5 support via live chat and email. Phone support is not available — this is one area where the lean operating model is visible. Live chat response times average 2-5 minutes with helpful, knowledgeable agents.

The support team is smaller than large competitors but compensates with quality. The 4.5 Trustpilot score suggests support interactions are generally positive. The knowledge base covers common questions and platform guides.

## Education

Fusion Markets provides minimal educational content — basic trading guides and market analysis. The education offering is not a strength and not suitable for complete beginners. Fusion Markets targets cost-conscious traders who already have trading knowledge.

For educational resources, beginners should look to IG Academy, Capital.com Investmate, or XTB's education section. Fusion Markets' value proposition is about saving money on execution, not learning to trade.
