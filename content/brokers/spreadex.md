## Overview

Spreadex is a uniquely British broker that has served UK traders exclusively since 1999. Founded by Jonathan Hufford in Dunstable and now headquartered in St Albans, Hertfordshire, Spreadex is the only FCA-regulated firm offering financial spread betting, CFD trading, and sports spread betting under a single account — a combination no other broker provides.

With over 60,000 active clients and £104 million in annual revenue, Spreadex has built a sustainable business focused entirely on the UK market. This UK-only focus means all operations, client funds, and regulatory oversight fall under full FCA jurisdiction — there are no offshore entities or secondary regulators to navigate.

Spreadex offers 10,000+ tradable instruments covering forex (60+ pairs), indices, shares, commodities, bonds, ETFs, and options. The proprietary platform is supplemented by a TradingView integration that provides institutional-grade charting with direct spread betting execution. While the platform range is narrower than competitors (no MT4/MT5), the TradingView integration largely compensates for advanced traders.

For UK traders whose primary goal is tax-free spread betting with a trusted, long-standing domestic broker, Spreadex delivers a clean, focused experience without the complexity of multi-entity global brokers. FSCS £85,000 protection, zero minimum deposit, and no inactivity fees round out a straightforward UK-first proposition.

## Account Intro

## Account Outro

All accounts come with zero minimum deposit, no inactivity fees, and access to the full range of 10,000+ instruments. Demo accounts are available for practice trading. The same login provides access to sports spread betting markets.

## Regulation

Spreadex holds FCA authorisation (reference: 190941) and has been regulated since its founding in 1999. As a UK-only broker with no international entities, Spreadex provides a clear regulatory picture — every client interaction falls under FCA oversight with no ambiguity about which entity or regulator applies.

UK clients benefit from FSCS protection up to £85,000 per person. Client funds are held in segregated accounts at Barclays and Lloyds, separate from Spreadex's operational funds. Negative balance protection is provided for all retail clients under FCA rules. Spreadex also holds a UK Gambling Commission licence (8835) for its sports betting operations.

## Costs

Spreadex operates on a spread-only model with zero commissions across all markets. EUR/USD spreads start from 0.6 pips (average around 0.9 pips), which is competitive for a market maker but wider than ECN alternatives like IC Markets (0.02 pips raw).

The key cost advantage for UK traders is tax efficiency: spread betting profits are completely tax-free under current HMRC rules. This effectively reduces the real cost of trading versus CFD alternatives where profits are subject to Capital Gains Tax. For UK traders making consistent profits, the tax saving far outweighs any spread difference.

There are no deposit fees, no withdrawal fees, no inactivity charges, and no account maintenance fees. Overnight financing applies to positions held past market close, as standard across all spread betting providers.
