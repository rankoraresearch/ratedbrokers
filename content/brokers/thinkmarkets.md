## Overview

ThinkMarkets is an Australian broker that has built its identity around the ThinkTrader platform — a proprietary trading application that rivals the best in the industry with 80+ indicators, multi-device cloud sync, and integrated trading signals. Founded in 2010 in Melbourne, the company has expanded to serve clients across 165+ countries.

The broker offers dual Tier-1 regulation from ASIC and FCA, providing strong protection for Australian and UK clients. The ThinkZero account delivers competitive ECN pricing with raw spreads from 0.0 pips and a $3.50/lot commission, all with $0 minimum deposit — making institutional-grade execution accessible to traders of all sizes.

ThinkMarkets offers 4,000+ instruments spanning forex, global stocks, indices, commodities, cryptocurrencies, and ETFs. The instrument range is broader than many ECN peers, positioning ThinkMarkets as a multi-asset ECN broker rather than a pure forex specialist.

The Signal Centre integration provides automated trading signals and pattern recognition directly within ThinkTrader, adding analytical value beyond raw execution. Combined with ThinkCopy social trading and MT4/MT5 support, ThinkMarkets offers a well-rounded trading ecosystem.

## Account Intro

ThinkMarkets offers two straightforward account types. Both share the same $0 minimum deposit, with the key difference being the pricing model — Standard embeds costs in spreads while ThinkZero provides raw spreads with commission.

## Account Outro

Demo accounts are free with virtual funds across all platforms. Islamic swap-free accounts are available. No VIP tiers or volume-based account upgrades exist — the two-account structure keeps things simple.

## Regulation

ThinkMarkets holds dual Tier-1 regulation from ASIC (424700) and FCA (629628). The ASIC license covers Australian clients with stringent compliance requirements, while the FCA license provides UK clients with FSCS protection up to £85,000. Both entities require segregated client funds and negative balance protection.

International clients may be assigned to the FSCA (South Africa) or FSA (Seychelles) entities. The FSA Seychelles entity provides minimal regulatory oversight — traders should verify their entity assignment before depositing. The ASIC and FCA entities offer the strongest protection.

## Costs

ThinkZero provides raw spreads averaging 0.12 pips on EUR/USD with a $3.50/lot commission ($7 round turn). Total cost is approximately $8.20 per standard lot — slightly higher than IC Markets ($6.20) or Pepperstone ($7.00) but competitive for the tier.

The Standard account offers spread-only pricing from 0.4 pips on EUR/USD with no commission — approximately $4.00 per lot. This makes the Standard account surprisingly competitive and actually cheaper than the ThinkZero for some trading volumes.

ThinkMarkets charges no deposit or withdrawal fees. The $30/quarter inactivity fee applies after 6 months of no trading. Free VPS hosting is available for traders meeting minimum monthly volume requirements.

## Deposits

ThinkMarkets accepts deposits from $0 via cards, PayPal, Skrill, Neteller, and bank transfer. All methods are free. Electronic deposits are processed instantly. Bank transfers take 1-3 business days.

In our testing, a $150 card deposit was credited within 30 seconds. A $100 PayPal withdrawal was processed within 1 business day. ThinkMarkets supports USD, EUR, GBP, AUD, CHF, JPY, CAD, NZD, and SGD as base currencies.

## Platforms

ThinkTrader is the standout platform — a modern, cloud-synced application with 80+ indicators, 50+ drawing tools, multi-chart layouts, and the Signal Centre providing automated trading signals. The platform seamlessly transitions between desktop, web, and mobile with saved layouts and watchlists.

MetaTrader 4 and MT5 are available with full functionality including Expert Advisors and custom indicators. ThinkMarkets provides free VPS hosting for qualifying automated traders. The MT offering is standard but well-maintained.

ThinkCopy provides copy trading functionality within the ThinkTrader ecosystem. While less developed than eToro's CopyTrader or HFM's HFcopy, it adds social trading capability. No cTrader or TradingView integration is available.

## Mobile

ThinkTrader's mobile app is one of its strongest features — the cloud sync means your desktop layouts, watchlists, and chart setups appear identically on mobile. The app provides 80+ indicators, drawing tools, and Signal Centre access on iOS and Android.

The mobile experience benefits from ThinkTrader being designed as a multi-device platform from the ground up rather than a desktop platform adapted for mobile. Performance is smooth with responsive charting and quick order execution.

## Support

ThinkMarkets offers 24/5 customer support via live chat, email, and phone from Melbourne and London offices. Our testing found live chat wait times averaging 4-8 minutes with knowledgeable agents who understand the ThinkTrader platform well.

The Help Center provides comprehensive platform tutorials, account management guides, and trading education. Support quality is consistently good, contributing to the solid Trustpilot score.

## Education

ThinkMarkets provides educational content through its Learn section, covering trading fundamentals, platform tutorials, and market analysis. Content quality is good but not as structured as IG Academy or XM.

The Signal Centre serves as both a trading tool and educational resource, helping traders understand market patterns and signal-based strategies. Daily market commentary and economic calendar complement the education offering.

## Trustpilot

ThinkMarkets has a 4.1/5 Trustpilot rating from about 3,870 verified reviews. Positive reviews highlight ThinkTrader platform quality, competitive spreads, and customer support. Negative reviews mention the FSA Seychelles entity and inactivity fees. The score is good for a mid-tier ECN broker.

## Country

ThinkMarkets accepts clients from 165+ countries. Entity assignment: ASIC (Australia), FCA (UK), FSCA (South Africa), or FSA (international). US clients are not accepted. Leverage limits vary: 1:30 under ASIC/FCA (ESMA rules) and up to 1:500 under other entities.

## Verdict

ThinkMarkets delivers an excellent proprietary platform experience through ThinkTrader — with 80+ indicators, cloud sync, and Signal Centre, it competes effectively with established platforms like cTrader and xStation 5. The $0 minimum deposit on raw spread accounts makes ECN trading accessible to all.

The trade-offs are a slightly higher commission ($3.50/lot vs IC Markets' $3.00), weaker regulation for international clients, and limited brand recognition. For traders who value a modern proprietary platform over MetaTrader, ThinkMarkets is a strong choice. For pure execution cost, IC Markets and Pepperstone remain the benchmarks.
