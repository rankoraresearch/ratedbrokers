## Overview

NAGA is a German fintech company that has built one of Europe's leading social trading platforms, combining traditional forex and CFD trading with social investing, cryptocurrency, and real stock ownership. Founded in 2015 by Benjamin Bilski and publicly listed on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange (N4G), NAGA operates with CySEC and BaFin dual Tier-1 regulation.

The platform's core proposition is social trading — users can browse, follow, and automatically copy trades from successful traders within the NAGA community. The social feed, discussion features, and transparent performance metrics create a genuine social investing experience that goes beyond simple trade copying.

With 3,500+ instruments including forex, real stocks, indices, commodities, and 50+ cryptocurrencies, NAGA provides broad market access. The integration of a crypto wallet alongside traditional trading accounts creates a unified platform for traders interested in both conventional and digital assets.

The 4.4/5 Trustpilot score from 3,250+ reviews reflects strong user satisfaction, particularly around the social features, mobile app quality, and copy trading implementation. As a publicly listed company, NAGA offers a transparency level rare among brokers — audited financials and corporate governance obligations provide additional confidence.

## Account Intro

NAGA offers three account tiers based on deposit level. All are commission-free with costs in the spread. Higher tiers receive tighter spreads, priority support, and enhanced copy trading features.

## Account Outro

Demo accounts with virtual funds are available. Islamic swap-free accounts can be requested. Copy trading operates on all account tiers. The NAGA Wallet for crypto can be used independently of trading accounts.

## Regulation

NAGA Group AG is regulated by CySEC (204/13) and registered with BaFin (135203) — dual Tier-1 regulators covering Cyprus and Germany respectively. As a publicly listed company on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange, NAGA publishes audited annual reports and adheres to German corporate governance standards.

Client funds are segregated in European banks. EU clients benefit from negative balance protection and ICF coverage up to €20,000. The FSCA South Africa license extends coverage to African clients. The public listing on a major stock exchange provides a transparency advantage unique among social trading platforms.

## Costs

NAGA operates a commission-free model with costs built into spreads. Iron account (min $50): EUR/USD 1.5 pips ≈ $15.00/lot. Gold ($2,500): 1.2 pips ≈ $12.00/lot. Diamond ($15,000): 0.7 pips ≈ $7.00/lot. These are wider than ECN brokers but standard for social trading platforms.

Copy trading fees vary by signal provider — typically 5-20% of profits generated from copied trades. This is an additional cost beyond spreads. Real stock purchases have separate commission structures depending on the exchange.

No deposit fees. No withdrawal fees for standard methods. Inactivity fee of $20/month after 3 months of no trading activity. The spread-inclusive model is simple to understand but more expensive for active traders compared to ECN alternatives.

## Deposits

NAGA accepts deposits from $50 via cards, bank transfer, Skrill, Neteller, crypto, and Apple Pay. All methods are free with no NAGA-imposed fees. Electronic deposits are instant; bank transfers take 1-3 business days.

In our testing, a card deposit was credited within 15 seconds. Crypto deposits via BTC were confirmed within 30 minutes. Withdrawals processed within 1-2 business days. NAGA supports USD, EUR, and GBP as base currencies.

## Platforms

The NAGA proprietary platform (web and mobile) is the flagship product, integrating trading, social feed, copy trading, and crypto wallet in a unified interface. The social feed shows real-time trades from community members, market discussions, and analyst insights — creating a social media-like experience around trading.

MetaTrader 4 and MT5 are available for traders who prefer traditional platforms with EA support. However, MT4/MT5 don't include the social features — those are exclusive to the NAGA platform. Most NAGA users prefer the proprietary platform for the social experience.

The NAGA Wallet provides cryptocurrency holding, sending, and receiving alongside the trading platform. This integration of a non-custodial crypto wallet within a regulated trading platform distinguishes NAGA from most competitors.

## Mobile

The NAGA mobile app integrates trading, social features, copy trading, and crypto wallet in a single interface. The app provides the full social feed, auto-copy configuration, portfolio analytics, and direct trading — a comprehensive mobile experience.

The app is well-rated on both App Store and Google Play. The social integration on mobile creates an engaging experience similar to social media apps. Standard MT4/MT5 mobile apps are also available but lack the social features.

## Support

NAGA provides 24/5 customer support via live chat, email, and phone. Multi-language support covers 10+ languages. Our testing found live chat wait times of 3-6 minutes with helpful agents, though technical depth was occasionally limited.

Gold and Diamond account holders receive priority support. The NAGA community itself provides peer support through the social feed. The Help Center covers common topics with guides and video tutorials.

## Education

NAGA provides educational content through the platform including trading guides, market analysis, and community-generated content. The social feed itself serves an educational function — beginners can learn by observing experienced traders' decisions, analyses, and discussions.

NAGA Academy offers structured courses on trading basics, technical analysis, and risk management. While not as comprehensive as IG Academy, the combination of formal education and social learning is effective for beginners entering markets through social trading.

## Verdict

NAGA occupies a unique position as a publicly listed, CySEC+BaFin regulated social trading platform. The combination of social investing, copy trading, real stock ownership, and integrated crypto wallet creates an all-in-one platform for traders who value community and social features. The public listing on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange adds transparency rare in the brokerage industry.

The trade-offs are clear: wider spreads (1.5 pips EUR/USD on Iron) make NAGA expensive for active individual traders, and the lack of ECN or raw spread options limits appeal for execution-focused traders. For social trading and copy investing, NAGA competes directly with eToro — offering stronger EU regulation but a smaller community. For pure trading execution, ECN brokers like IC Markets or Pepperstone are far more cost-effective.
