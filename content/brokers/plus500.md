## Overview

Plus500 is a publicly traded CFD broker listed on the London Stock Exchange (LSE: PLUS), making it one of the most transparent and accountable brokers in the industry. Founded in 2008 in Haifa, Israel, by six Technion graduates, the company has grown to serve 400,000+ active clients with 2,800+ CFD instruments.

Plus500's approach is simplicity-first. The proprietary platform is designed to be intuitive and easy to use, with clean design and straightforward order placement. This makes it appealing for casual and part-time traders who don't need the complexity of MetaTrader or cTrader. The trade-off is minimal charting tools and no advanced analysis features.

The broker offers CFDs exclusively — there is no real stock ownership, DMA access, or spread betting. Instruments span forex, stocks, indices, commodities, ETFs, options, and cryptocurrencies, all traded as contracts for difference. A unique feature is guaranteed stop-loss orders, which protect against slippage during volatile markets.

Plus500 holds four Tier-1 regulatory licenses (FCA, CySEC, ASIC, MAS) and its LSE listing provides additional oversight through public market disclosure requirements. The company's market capitalization exceeds $1.5 billion, reflecting institutional confidence.

## Account Intro

Plus500 keeps it simple with one main account type for retail traders and a Professional account for qualified clients seeking higher leverage.

## Account Outro

Demo accounts are unlimited and free. There are no ECN, raw spread, or tiered account options. Islamic swap-free accounts are not available. The single account structure reflects Plus500's simplicity-focused approach.

## Regulation

Plus500 holds four Tier-1 licenses: FCA (509909), CySEC (250/14), ASIC (417727), and MAS (CMS100648). As a publicly traded company on the LSE, Plus500 is subject to additional regulatory scrutiny including mandatory financial disclosures, independent auditing, and corporate governance standards.

Client funds are segregated under all entities with negative balance protection. The FCA entity provides FSCS coverage up to £85,000. The combination of four Tier-1 licenses and public company status makes Plus500 one of the safest brokers available.

## Costs

Plus500 offers zero-commission trading with all costs embedded in the spread. EUR/USD averages 0.70 pips — competitive for a market maker and tighter than eToro (1.0 pips) or XM (0.80 pips). Total cost per standard lot is approximately $7.00.

The hidden cost factors are overnight funding fees (applied daily to open positions), currency conversion fees (0.7%), and the $10/month inactivity fee after 3 months. For swing and position traders, overnight fees can add up significantly.

Guaranteed stop-loss orders carry a wider spread premium shown transparently before order confirmation. While this adds cost, the protection against gap and slippage risk is valuable for risk-conscious traders.

## Deposits

Plus500 supports standard deposit methods with a $100 minimum. Cards, PayPal, Skrill, and Apple Pay are processed instantly with no fees. Bank transfers take 1-3 business days.

In our testing, a $300 card deposit was credited within 30 seconds. A $200 PayPal withdrawal was processed within 1 business day. Plus500 supports 14 base currencies including USD, EUR, GBP, AUD, and CHF.

## Platforms

Plus500 offers a single proprietary platform available as a web application and mobile app. The platform prioritizes simplicity with a clean interface, intuitive navigation, and quick order placement. Approximately 15 technical indicators are available — significantly fewer than MT4/MT5 or competing proprietary platforms.

There is no MetaTrader, cTrader, TradingView, or any third-party platform integration. No Expert Advisors, no API trading, no advanced order types beyond standard and guaranteed stops. This is a platform for manual, discretionary trading only.

The guaranteed stop-loss feature is a notable addition — Plus500 is one of the few brokers offering this protection, which guarantees your stop will be executed at the specified price even during gaps or extreme volatility.

## Mobile

The Plus500 mobile app is one of its strongest features — clean, fast, and intuitive with full trading functionality. Real-time alerts, watchlists, and one-tap trading make mobile management easy. The app consistently receives high ratings in iOS and Android stores.

For casual traders who primarily monitor and manage positions on mobile, Plus500's app provides a good experience. However, the limited charting and analysis tools remain a constraint even on mobile.

## Support

Plus500 offers 24/7 customer support via live chat and email. There is no phone support. In our testing, live chat responses averaged 5-10 minutes with adequate resolution quality. Support agents are helpful but less technically knowledgeable than at specialized brokers.

The Help Center provides searchable articles covering account management, platform usage, and trading basics. For complex trading questions, the lack of phone support and limited agent expertise can be frustrating.

## Education

Plus500 provides minimal educational content. The Trader's Guide covers basic concepts, and the platform includes a glossary. There are no video courses, webinars, live training sessions, or structured educational programs.

For beginners who need education, eToro, XM, or IG are significantly better choices. Plus500 targets traders who already understand CFDs and want a simple execution platform rather than a learning environment.

## Trustpilot

Plus500 has a 4.0/5 Trustpilot rating from over 15,000 verified reviews. Positive reviews highlight platform simplicity and regulatory safety. Negative reviews focus on the inactivity fee, limited platform features, overnight fees, and the lack of educational content. The polarized ratings reflect the broker's narrow appeal to simplicity-seeking traders.

## Country

Plus500 accepts clients from 50+ countries across FCA (UK), CySEC (EU), ASIC (Australia), MAS (Singapore), and other entities. US clients can access Plus500 Futures (CFTC-regulated). Notable exclusions include most of Africa, much of Asia, and several EU countries.

## Verdict

Plus500 is a safe, simple CFD broker backed by four Tier-1 licenses and an LSE listing. For casual traders who want straightforward CFD trading with guaranteed stops and don't need advanced analysis tools, Plus500 delivers a clean, no-fuss experience.

However, the limited platform, minimal education, $10 inactivity fee, and absence of MT4/MT5 make it hard to recommend over competitors like IG (better platform and education), eToro (copy trading), or Capital.com (AI-driven analysis). Plus500 fills a niche for simplicity seekers but lacks the depth most traders eventually need.
